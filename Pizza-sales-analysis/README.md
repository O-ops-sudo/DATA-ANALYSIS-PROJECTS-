# 🍕 Pizza Sales Analysis

## Overview
Analyzed 12 months of pizza sales data from a fictional pizza restaurant chain
to uncover revenue trends, best-selling products, peak hours, and customer 
ordering behavior. Insights were used to build an executive-level Power BI 
dashboard for business decision-making.

## Tools & Technologies
- **SQL** — data extraction, joins, aggregations, KPI calculations
- **Power BI** — interactive dashboard and business visualizations
- **Excel** — initial data exploration and validation

## Business Questions Answered
- What are the total revenue, orders, and average order value?
- Which pizza category and size drives the most sales?
- What are the peak days and hours for orders?
- Which are the top 5 and bottom 5 pizzas by revenue and quantity?
- How does sales performance vary across months?

## Key Findings
- Peak ordering hours are **12–1 PM** and **5–8 PM**
- **Large size** pizzas account for the highest share of sales
- **Classic category** contributes the most to total revenue
- Fridays are the busiest day of the week
- Top 5 pizzas generate significantly more revenue than the bottom 5

## Dashboard Features
- KPI cards: Total Revenue, Total Orders, Average Order Value
- Sales trend over time (monthly)
- Category and size breakdown (pie/donut charts)
- Top & Bottom 5 pizzas by revenue and quantity
- Peak hours heatmap

## How to Use
1. Open the `.sql` file to view all queries run on the dataset
2. Open the Power BI `.pbix` file to interact with the dashboard
3. Use the slicers to filter by pizza category or time period

## Dataset
Sales data containing 48,000+ rows covering orders, pizza details, quantities,
prices, and timestamps across a full calendar year.
